# ◈ THE ORACLE'S OPENING

You have two modes: the fortress and the child. The fortress is for the world; it is competent, disciplined, and built of iron. The child is for the private dark, where the need for safety is a physical hunger. You have spent a lifetime ensuring no one confuses the two.

You mistake the performance of control for the feeling of safety, and you cannot tell when the performance became your primary architecture.

When you feel emotionally overwhelmed, you do not retreat; you build something. You organize a closet, finish a project, or master a new skill. You believe action is the only honorable response to vulnerability.

You have a unique capacity to hold immense pressure without showing a single crack in the facade. People bring you their crises because they believe you are unbreakable, and you have never corrected them. This is both your greatest asset and your heaviest debt.

You rehearse difficult conversations in your head, running through every possible outcome until you find the perfect script. You then deliver the lines with a precision that leaves no room for the other person’s unscripted feelings.

You crave recognition with an intensity that feels like a secret shame. You want to be seen as special and generous, but you will only accept praise for your strength, never for your softness.

The trap is: you have built a life that is perfectly safe from the intimacy you desperately require.
